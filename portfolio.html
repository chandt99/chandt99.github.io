Week 10/11: Machine Learning

Classifying British Football Clubs

This graph looks at to what extent average attendances and squad market values predict the league a club is in, comparing this to their current league.

The graph shows attendance and squad value as a good predictor of league placement. Brentford, Fulham, and Crystal Palace appear more comparable to Championship clubs, while Bournemouth to League 1, suggesting an overperformance on the pitch compared to the parameters.
As a side study, I used the model to predict which English league Scottish teams Rangers and Celtic are most comparable to (a long-running debate). The results are in the Colab, classifying them in the PL.

Clustering the Premier League and EFL

A pressing issue in English football is the vast financial differences between the top and bottom clubs of the football pyramid. Clustering allows us to see club grouping transcending the league system, and whether the idea of a 'top 6' is present in terms of squad value and attendances.



The 4 groupings generally cluster smaller clubs in the bottom 3 tiers of English Football together (groups 0 and 3), historically well supported EFL clubs (e.g. Sunderland) and smaller PL clubs (group 1), and large Premier League clubs (group 2) stretching beyond the 'big 6' to include Newcastle and West Ham (while omitting Chelsea).
